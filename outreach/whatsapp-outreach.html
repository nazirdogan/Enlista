WhatsApp Outreach Strategy
When to use WhatsApp, what makes it different from email, and the rules that keep it effective.
📱
When WhatsApp beats email
UAE is a WhatsApp-first business culture. Founders and CEOs regularly handle deals on WhatsApp. For direct mobile numbers — especially where no email exists — it's often the faster and more natural channel.
⏱️
Send window
Tuesday–Thursday, 10 AM–12 PM Gulf Standard Time. Avoid Monday mornings (inbox overload), Friday afternoons (prayer/wind-down), and weekends unless you've already established contact.
📏
Message length
Keep it to 3–5 lines max on mobile. If it requires scrolling, it's too long. WhatsApp is a conversation medium — write like a peer, not a pitch deck. No bullet points. No headers.
🔢
Sequence limit
3 messages maximum to a cold contact. More than that crosses into harassment. Respect the channel. If they haven't replied after message 3, move to email or LinkedIn and do not WhatsApp again.
🎙️
No voice notes cold
Voice notes are for established relationships only. Cold voice notes feel intrusive and have near-zero engagement. Text only for initial outreach — voice is fine once they've replied.
💼
Use Business WhatsApp
Set up a WhatsApp Business account for ListingAI with your company name, description, and website. This builds credibility and lets contacts verify who they're talking to before replying.
Do's and Don'ts
WhatsApp outreach fails differently from email. These are the rules that matter most.
Do
  • Use a real name — "Nazir from ListingAI"
  • Lead with their business, not your product
  • Keep the ask ultra-low-friction ("worth a quick call?")
  • Send at 10 AM–12 PM GST, Tue–Thu
  • Wait at least 4 days before follow-up
  • Use Business WhatsApp with a complete profile
  • Stop after 3 messages if no reply
  • Honour any "not interested" response permanently
  • Reference something specific to their agency
Don't
  • Send voice notes cold
  • Open with "Hi, my name is X and I work at Y"
  • Use bullet points or headers in messages
  • Send the same message as your email (they'll notice)
  • Follow up more than twice after initial contact
  • Send on Friday afternoon or weekends
  • Add them to a WhatsApp group without consent
  • Send media files (images, PDFs) in cold outreach
  • Use "I hope this message finds you well"

Reply Handling
Suggested responses for every reply scenario — reply within 2 hours for any positive response.
Speed matters: A founder who replies to a cold WhatsApp is a hot signal. Respond within 2 hours. If you're not available to follow up quickly, don't send the message yet.
Reply Scenarios
✓ Positive — "Interested"
Great, are you free for a 15-min call this week? Here's a link: [Calendly] Or if it's easier, I can send a 2-min video showing it in action first.
→ Book demo immediately. Respond within 2 hours. Move to demo-booked sequence.
⏳ Not now — "Follow up later"
Of course, no problem. I'll check back in a few months. Good luck with the listings in the meantime.
→ Add to 90-day re-queue. Mark date. Remove from active sequence.
✗ Not interested
Understood, thanks for letting me know. Good luck with [Agency Name].
→ Do not contact again. Mark as do-not-contact in your list. Honour it.
❓ Question — "How does it work?"
Happy to show you. It connects to your existing listings and generates the full description — tailored copy, portal-ready format — in about 90 seconds. I can walk you through it live in 10 minutes or send a quick video. Which is easier?
→ Offer both options. Let them choose the format. Get on a call if possible.
💰 Pricing question
Happy to share that. Pricing is based on listing volume — it's designed to be affordable for boutique agencies. Worth a quick call to go through the numbers?
→ Don't quote pricing over WhatsApp if avoidable. Get them on a call where you can address objections.
🤔 "We already have a tool"
Good to know — what are you using? Most tools handle the CRM side but leave the portal copy writing to agents. That's typically where the time goes.
→ Ask a genuine question. If they have full XML automation with AI copy, disengage gracefully. If there's a gap, keep going.
