My Recommendation

Start with Structure 1.
Build toward Structure 3.

ListingAI is at MVP stage with a solid quota system already in place. Flat-Rate Tiers (Structure 1) is the fastest path to paid revenue — it matches the existing codebase, requires minimal billing complexity, and gives you clean conversion data to inform future pricing.

Once team features are shipped (6–9 months), migrate the Agency tier into a per-seat model (Structure 3). This is how Notion, Linear, and Loom all scaled — simple personal plans that convert to high-ACV team contracts.

Avoid Usage-Based (Structure 2) as a starting point — unpredictable revenue and a free tier creates support overhead before you have the team to handle it.

  • Matches existing code quota logic

Final Verdict

Structure 3 is the right model.
Structure 1 is the right launch.

Structure 3 — per-agent pricing with individual accounts under a brokerage umbrella — is the correct long-term architecture. It mirrors how the UAE market already thinks (PropSpace), it captures revenue growth automatically as agencies hire, and it gives agents the individual ownership they want while giving brokerages the oversight they need.

But team features aren't built yet. Structure 1's Solo plan ($49/mo) gets you to revenue immediately with zero code changes. Launch it, collect real customers, and use that MRR to fund building the umbrella dashboard. The Solo plan is identical in both structures — so early customers never need to be re-priced.
